Key Projects

Thermal Energy Storage Using Sand

Final Year Research Project | JKUAT | 2024-2025

Objective: To assess sand's potential as a low-cost, sustainable thermal energy storage medium for solar thermal systems.

Key Activities:

  • Conducted comprehensive literature review on thermal energy storage materials
  • Designed and conducted experiments to evaluate sand's heat retention properties
  • Analyzed thermal conductivity and heat capacity data
  • Evaluated cost-effectiveness and sustainability compared to conventional storage materials
  • Prepared detailed technical report with findings and recommendations

Outcomes: Successfully demonstrated sand as a viable, low-cost alternative for thermal energy storage in solar applications, particularly suitable for rural and resource-constrained settings.
